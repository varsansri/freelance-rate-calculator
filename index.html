Freelance Rate Calculator — FAQ

How do I calculate my freelance hourly rate?

Take your desired take-home pay, reverse the tax rate to get gross needed, add business expenses, then divide by annual billable hours. This tool does all that math instantly — just enter your numbers.

What is a good freelance hourly rate in 2024?

It depends on your skill and market. Entry-level freelancers charge $25–50/hr. Mid-level specialists charge $75–150/hr. Senior consultants charge $150–500+/hr. Calculate your minimum first, then price based on value delivered.

Why should freelancers charge more than employees?

Freelancers pay self-employment tax (15–30% extra), cover their own benefits, buy their own software and gear, handle unpaid admin time, and have no paid sick days or vacation. A $50/hr freelancer often earns less than a $35/hr employee after all costs.

How many billable hours do freelancers actually work per year?

Realistically 4–6 billable hours per day, not 8. The rest goes to admin, proposals, marketing, and non-billable tasks. With 6 billable hours and 2 weeks off, that's ~1,500 hours/year. Use realistic numbers for an accurate rate.

Should I charge per hour or per project as a freelancer?

Project pricing is better once you're experienced — it rewards your efficiency. Hourly works when scope is unclear. Either way, your hourly rate is your floor: never take a project that pays less than your minimum × estimated hours.

How to Calculate Your Freelance Hourly Rate (The Right Way)

Most freelancers price themselves by looking at competitors or guessing a number that "feels right." This leads to either undercharging (losing money after taxes) or overcharging (losing clients). The correct approach is to calculate your rate from your actual financial needs.

This freelance hourly rate calculator works backwards from your desired income goal. It accounts for self-employment tax, business expenses, realistic billable hours, and vacation time — giving you three rates: the minimum you need to break even, a comfortable rate with a 30% buffer, and a premium rate for specialist work.

The most important insight: if you're charging less than your calculated minimum rate, your freelance business is operating at a loss — even if your bank account looks healthy short-term. Use this calculator before setting your next client proposal.
